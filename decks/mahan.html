Alfred Mahan

Concept of Desicive Battle as a Way to Ensure Your Fleet's Defeat

Naval Officer

Born in 1840

Joined the fleet in 1859

Ended his service in 1885 in the rank of Captain

Lecturer

Became a lecturer at Naval War College in 1885

Continued working there until 1896

Author of Books

Mahan published 4 books that made him world-renowned authority in everything naval

The Influence of Sea Power upon History, 1660–1783

The Influence of Sea Power upon the French Revolution and Empire, 1793–1812

The Life of Nelson, the Embodiment of the Sea Power of Great Britain

Sea Power in Relation to the War of 1812

Impact

Mahan's theories found resonance with German, French and Japanese leadership, influencing their naval strategies for decades to come

Impact

Was embraced by Theodore Roosevelt

Mahan formed a personal frienship with him during 1890s

And corresponded with Franklin Delano until 1914

Impact

Intellectual justification for U.S. imperialist expansion overseas

Navy is the key instrument of defence and foreign policy

Impact

Promoted by Alfred von Tirpitz et al

Coincided with active development of German Imperial Fleet

Became basis for Germany's naval strategy in World War I

Impact

Kept seeking decisive battle with Austria-Hungary's fleet

Development of submarines was deferred

Impact
